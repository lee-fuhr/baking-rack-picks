Baking Rack questions answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

A baking rack, also called a cooling rack, is an essential tool for even cooling, roasting, and crisping. Look for sturdy stainless steel construction, a tight grid pattern, and raised feet that allow air to circulate freely. Here’s what to consider before buying one.

What is a baking rack and why do I need one?

A baking rack is a wire grid set on small legs that lifts food off the pan or countertop. Its main job is to let air circulate all around whatever you’ve baked, roasted, or fried, so heat and moisture escape evenly. Without it, the bottom of cookies, cakes, or roasted vegetables can steam and turn soggy. Beyond cooling, a rack inside a sheet pan turns it into a mini-oven: air moves under the food, making roast chicken skin crispier, bacon less greasy, and reheated pizza crust crunchy. It’s a small tool that punches well above its weight in the kitchen.

Which material is best for a baking rack?

Stainless steel is the gold standard because it doesn’t rust, warp, or react with acidic foods. Racks with a chrome-plated steel core are cheaper but can flake over time, especially in the dishwasher. Uncoated aluminum is lightweight and conducts heat well, but it can bend and react with ingredients like tomatoes, leaving a metallic taste. A rack with a non-stick coating is convenient for cleanup, but the coating can scratch or peel with heavy use. If you bake frequently, a simple, uncoated stainless steel rack from a reputable brand gives you the best balance of durability and performance.

What size baking rack should I buy?

Size matters because your rack needs to fit inside your sheet pan or directly on the counter. Most standard racks are designed for half-sheet pans (roughly 18 by 13 inches), but you’ll also find quarter-sheet sizes for smaller ovens and “jelly roll” pans. Measure your largest baking sheet before shopping, and leave a tiny gap around the edges so the rack sits flat. If you have multiple pan sizes, consider buying racks in two sizes or an adjustable rack with folding sides. Just know that adjustable racks often have a seam in the middle that can leave a line on softer baked goods.

What does grid pattern and spacing affect?

A tight grid pattern, small squares less than half an inch apart, is best for cooling cookies, muffins, and cakes because it prevents them from sagging between the wires. Wider spacing works fine for large roasts or whole chickens, but smaller items will fall through or dent. Grids with a series of parallel wires (rather than a full crosshatch) are common and work well, but they offer less support for delicate pastries. For all-purpose use, look for a rack with a dense, square-pattern grid. The wires should feel thick enough to resist bending when you lift a heavy roast.

Can I put my baking rack in the dishwasher?

It depends on the material. Uncoated stainless steel racks can go in the dishwasher without issue, just make sure they are truly stainless, not chrome-plated. Chromed racks often say “dishwasher safe” but the plating can pit or peel after a few cycles, especially if detergent touches exposed metal. Non-stick racks should always be hand washed to preserve the coating. A quick scrub with warm soapy water and a soft sponge is all they need. If you hate hand-washing, invest in a solid stainless steel rack and treat it well: it will last for years.

Do I need a special rack for roasting vs. cooling?

Not necessarily, most metal racks serve both purposes. But if you roast frequently, a slightly taller rack (with higher legs) is helpful because it lifts the food further above the pan, allowing hot air to circulate even better. Standard cooling racks have legs about half an inch tall, which is fine; roasting racks often go up to an inch. A rack with a very non-stick coating can be handy for sticky glazes, but the coating may not withstand high oven temperatures above 450°F. Check the manufacturer’s recommended temp limit. For high-heat roasting, plain stainless steel is the safest bet.

Frequently asked questions

Do I really need a baking rack, or can I just use a towel?

A towel or paper towels trap steam against the baked goods, making the bottom wet and soft. A rack lets air flow freely, so cookies and cakes cool evenly and stay crisp. It’s a cheap upgrade that makes a real difference.

What’s the difference between a cooling rack and a roasting rack?

The main difference is leg height and wire thickness. Roasting racks often have taller legs to keep food above the pan juices, and thicker wires to support heavy meats. Cooling racks are slightly shorter and finer, ideal for pastries, but either can be used for both jobs.

Can I use a baking rack directly on the oven rack?

Yes, but it’s usually not necessary. A rack on a sheet pan gives better heat circulation under the food and catches drips. If you place a rack directly on the oven shelf, clean-up is harder, and small items may slide through the oven grates.

How do I clean a stuck-on mess from a baking rack?

Soak the rack in hot water with a squirt of dish soap and a sprinkle of baking soda for about 20 minutes, then scrub gently with a stiff nylon brush. For burnt-on residue, make a paste of baking soda and water, let it sit, then scrub. Avoid steel wool on coated racks.

Is an aluminum baking rack safe to use?

Plain aluminum racks are lightweight and conduct heat well, but they can warp under high heat and react with acidic foods like lemon or tomato, giving a metallic taste. They’re fine for low-acid tasks and occasional use, but stainless steel is safer for regular baking.

Can I stack cooling racks to save space?

Stacking racks is fine for storage, but don’t use them stacked while cooling food because air can’t circulate between layers, defeating the purpose. Look for racks with folding sides or buy nesting sizes to keep your cupboard organized.

In shortA good baking rack won’t make headlines, but it will quietly improve everything you take out of the oven. Focus on stainless steel, a tight grid, and a size that fits your pans. Skip fancy extras you don’t need, and you’ll have a kitchen workhorse for years.
